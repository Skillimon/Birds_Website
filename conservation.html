Conservation: what matters and how to help

Practical guidance on threats to birds, conservation successes, and clear actions you can take in your garden, locally and nationally.

Why conservation matters

Birds are key indicators of environmental health. Protecting birds preserves biodiversity, supports pollination and pest control, and enriches our natural heritage.

Key threats to birds

  • Habitat loss: urban development, intensive agriculture and wetland drainage reduce nesting and feeding sites.
  • Pollution & pesticides: reduce insect prey and cause poisoning (avoid garden pesticides).
  • Invasive species: mink, rats and some plants can displace native wildlife.
  • Climate change: shifts migration and breeding timing, affecting food availability and habitats.
  • Disturbance: recreational pressure, especially at nesting sites and coastal colonies.

In your garden

  • Create shelter: keep hedges, ivy and dense native shrubs.
  • Provide food & water: seed mixes, suet, and a clean bird bath.
  • Plant for wildlife: berries, nectar plants and native trees (see Garden tips).
  • Limit chemicals: avoid pesticides to keep insect food chains intact.
  • Install nest boxes and maintain them annually.

Locally

  • Volunteer with local reserves or conservation groups (RSPB, local wildlife trusts).
  • Join citizen science schemes (BTO surveys, eBird) to contribute sightings.
  • Support habitat restoration projects (wetland creation, hedgerow restoration).

National / policy

  • Support policies that protect habitats and reduce emissions.
  • Vote for nature-friendly planning and agriculture policies.

Legal protections & success stories

Many species benefit from legal protections (e.g., SSSIs, protected nesting sites) and successful conservation efforts have recovered species such as the peregrine and some seabirds. Local habitat protection and targeted species programmes continue to be important.
