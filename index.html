Bird of the Month

Turnstone Arenaria interpres

The Turnstone is a small, robust wader famous for its habit of flipping stones and seaweed to uncover food. Often seen along rocky shores, it’s a passage migrant in the UK, delighting birdwatchers during spring and autumn migration.

  • Habitat: Rocky shores and coastal beaches
  • Migration: Passage migrant in the UK
  • Lifespan: up to 10-15 years
  • Diet: Crustaceans, insects, worms and molluscs
  • Name origin: Known for flipping stones and seaweed to find food
  • UK sightings: Often seen during spring and autumn migration
